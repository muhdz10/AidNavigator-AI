# Supplemental Security Income (SSI)

## Program Overview

The Supplemental Security Income (SSI) program is administered by the Social Security Administration (SSA). It provides monthly payments to adults and children with a disability or blindness who have income and resources below specific financial limits. SSI payments are also made to people aged 65 and older without disabilities who meet the financial qualifications.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Categorical Eligibility
To qualify for SSI, you must be one of the following:
- Age 65 or older.
- Blind (any age).
- Disabled (any age).

### Financial Eligibility

#### Income Limits
Your income is money you receive such as wages, Social Security benefits, and pensions. Income also includes things like food and shelter you get for free or less than fair market value. The amount of income you can receive each month and still get SSI depends partly on where you live.

#### Resource Limits
The limit for countable resources is:
- $2,000 for individuals.
- $3,000 for couples.
*Note: Not all resources count. For example, your primary home and one vehicle generally do not count towards this limit.*

### Residential and Citizenship Eligibility
- Must reside in one of the 50 states, the District of Columbia, or the Northern Mariana Islands.
- Must be a U.S. citizen or national, or an eligible noncitizen.

## Required Documentation
When applying, you may need:
1. Proof of age (birth certificate).
2. Proof of citizenship or legal alien status.
3. Proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns).
4. Proof of resources (bank statements, vehicle registrations).
5. Medical records (if applying based on disability or blindness).

## How to Apply
You can begin the SSI application process online at the SSA website, by calling the SSA toll-free number, or by visiting your local Social Security office.
