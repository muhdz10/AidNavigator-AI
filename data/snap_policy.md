# Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)

## Program Overview

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), formerly known as the Food Stamp Program, is the largest federal nutrition assistance program. Administered by the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Food and Nutrition Service (FNS), SNAP provides monthly benefits to eligible low-income individuals and families to purchase food at authorized retail stores.

## Federal Eligibility Requirements

### Income Limits

SNAP eligibility is based on both gross and net income tests:

- **Gross Monthly Income**: Must be at or below **130% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL)**. For a household of 1, this is approximately $1,580/month ($18,954/year). For a household of 4, this is approximately $3,250/month ($39,000/year).
- **Net Monthly Income**: After allowable deductions, net income must be at or below **100% of the FPL**. For a household of 1, this is approximately $1,215/month ($14,580/year). For a household of 4, this is approximately $2,500/month ($30,000/year).
- **Elderly/Disabled Exception**: Households containing an elderly member (age 60+) or a person with a disability only need to meet the net income test, not the gross income test. Some states allow up to 200% FPL for these households.

### Resource Limits

- **Standard households**: Countable resources (bank accounts, cash) must not exceed **$3,000**.
- **Households with elderly/disabled members**: Countable resources must not exceed **$4,500**.
- **Excluded resources**: Primary home, personal belongings, one vehicle (varies by state), retirement accounts, and education savings accounts are generally excluded.

### Household Size and Composition

A SNAP household includes all people who live together and purchase and prepare meals together. Spouses and children under age 22 living with parents must be in the same household application regardless of meal preparation arrangements.

## Work Requirements

- **General work requirement**: Able-bodied adults ages 16–59 must register for work, accept suitable employment, and not voluntarily quit a job without good cause.
- **ABAWD requirements**: Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWDs) ages 18–54 must work or participate in a qualifying work program at least 80 hours per month. Without meeting this requirement, benefits are limited to 3 months in a 36-month period.
- **Exemptions**: Individuals who are pregnant, caring for a child under 6, medically certified as physically or mentally unfit, or participating in a drug/alcohol treatment program are exempt.

## Benefit Amounts

Maximum monthly SNAP benefits (fiscal year 2024-2025):
- 1 person: $292
- 2 persons: $536
- 3 persons: $768
- 4 persons: $975
- 5 persons: $1,158
- Each additional person: approximately $183

## Required Documentation

To apply for SNAP, applicants typically need:
1. Proof of identity (government-issued photo ID)
2. Proof of residency (utility bill, lease agreement, or mail)
3. Social Security numbers for all household members
4. Proof of income (pay stubs, employer letter, tax return)
5. Proof of resources (bank statements)
6. Proof of housing costs (rent receipt, mortgage statement)
7. Proof of dependent care expenses (if applicable)
8. Medical expense documentation (for elderly/disabled members)

## How to Apply

Applications can be submitted through:
- Your state's SNAP office (in person)
- Online through your state's benefits portal
- By mail or fax
- Through authorized community organizations

Processing typically takes up to 30 days, or 7 days for expedited service if the household has extremely low income or resources.

## Important Notes

- SNAP is a federal program administered by individual states, which may have additional rules or broader eligibility through "broad-based categorical eligibility."
- Benefits are provided on an Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) card.
- Eligibility must be recertified periodically (typically every 6–12 months).
- Undocumented immigrants are not eligible, but eligible household members can receive benefits regardless of the immigration status of other household members.

Source: USDA Food and Nutrition Service (https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap)
