# Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program

## Program Overview

The Housing Choice Voucher Program (commonly known as Section 8) is the federal government's primary program for assisting very low-income families, the elderly, and persons with disabilities to afford decent, safe, and sanitary housing in the private market. Administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), the program is operated locally by approximately 2,200 Public Housing Agencies (PHAs) across the country.

## How the Program Works

- Participants find their own housing in the private market, including single-family homes, townhouses, and apartments.
- The PHA pays a housing subsidy (the "voucher") directly to the landlord on behalf of the participating family.
- The family pays the difference between the actual rent charged by the landlord and the amount subsidized by the program.
- Generally, families are expected to pay **30% of their adjusted monthly income** toward rent and utilities.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Income Limits

Eligibility is determined primarily by total annual gross income and family size. Income limits are set by HUD and vary by metropolitan area:

- **Very Low Income (VLI)**: Families whose income does not exceed **50% of the Area Median Income (AMI)**. By law, PHAs must provide 75% of their vouchers to families at or below 30% AMI.
- **Extremely Low Income (ELI)**: Families whose income does not exceed **30% of AMI** or the federal poverty level, whichever is higher.
- **Low Income**: Families whose income does not exceed **80% of AMI** (limited eligibility).

### Example Income Limits (National Approximation, 2024-2025)

| Family Size | 30% AMI (Extremely Low) | 50% AMI (Very Low) | 80% AMI (Low) |
|------------|------------------------|--------------------|--------------| 
| 1 person | ~$15,000-$22,000 | ~$25,000-$37,000 | ~$40,000-$59,000 |
| 2 persons | ~$17,000-$25,000 | ~$29,000-$42,000 | ~$46,000-$68,000 |
| 3 persons | ~$19,000-$28,000 | ~$32,000-$47,000 | ~$51,000-$76,000 |
| 4 persons | ~$21,000-$31,000 | ~$36,000-$53,000 | ~$57,000-$84,000 |

*Note: Actual limits vary widely by location. Urban areas with higher cost of living have higher AMI thresholds.*

### Additional Requirements

- **Citizenship**: At least one member of the household must be a U.S. citizen or eligible noncitizen.
- **Background check**: PHAs conduct criminal background checks. Individuals subject to lifetime sex offender registration or convicted of manufacturing methamphetamine on federally assisted property are permanently ineligible.
- **Rental history**: PHAs may consider prior rental history and whether applicants owe money to another PHA.
- **Family composition**: A family can be a single person or a group of related or unrelated people who live together as a family unit.

## Priority Categories

PHAs may establish local preferences for selection, commonly including:
- Families that are homeless or living in substandard housing
- Families paying more than 50% of their income for rent
- Families that have been involuntarily displaced
- Veterans and active military families
- Families with elderly or disabled members
- Victims of domestic violence

## Waiting Lists

Due to high demand, most PHAs maintain waiting lists. Wait times vary dramatically:
- **Typical wait time**: 1 to 5 years in most areas
- **High-demand areas**: Wait lists may be closed or have 5-10+ year waits
- PHAs periodically open their waiting lists for new applications

## Required Documentation

To apply for Section 8, applicants typically need:
1. Government-issued photo identification for all adult household members
2. Social Security cards for all household members
3. Birth certificates for all household members
4. Proof of income (pay stubs, benefit award letters, tax returns)
5. Proof of assets (bank statements, investment records)
6. Proof of current address and rental history
7. Documentation of any disabilities (if claiming preference)
8. Proof of veteran status (if claiming preference)
9. Documentation of citizenship or eligible immigration status

## Tenant Responsibilities

Once receiving a voucher, tenants must:
- Find housing that meets HUD's Housing Quality Standards (HQS)
- Pay their portion of rent on time
- Maintain the housing unit in good condition
- Report changes in income or family composition to the PHA
- Comply with the terms of the lease
- Not engage in criminal activity

## Important Notes

- The Section 8 program does NOT guarantee housing — it provides financial assistance to make private-market housing affordable.
- Vouchers are "portable" — families can generally move to any area where a PHA administers the program.
- Landlord participation is voluntary; landlords are not required to accept vouchers (except where state or local law requires it).
- Income eligibility is recertified annually.
- The subsidy amount is based on a "payment standard" set by the PHA, which is between 90% and 110% of the HUD Fair Market Rent for the area.
