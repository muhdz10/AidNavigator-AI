# Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)

## Program Overview

The Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP) is a federally funded program that helps low-income households meet their home energy needs. Administered by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) through the Administration for Children and Families (ACF), LIHEAP provides grants to states, territories, and tribes, which then distribute assistance to eligible households. The program helps with heating costs, cooling costs, energy crises, weatherization, and energy-related home repairs.

## Types of Assistance

### 1. Heating Assistance
- Helps pay winter heating bills
- Available typically from October/November through March/April (varies by state)
- Can be provided as a one-time payment or spread across the heating season
- Payment is usually made directly to the energy vendor/utility company

### 2. Cooling Assistance
- Helps pay summer cooling/air conditioning costs
- Available typically from May through September (varies by state)
- Some states also provide fans or air conditioning units

### 3. Energy Crisis Assistance
- Emergency assistance for households facing energy emergencies
- Situations include: utility shutoff, broken heating/cooling equipment, running out of fuel
- Available year-round in most states
- Typically provides faster processing than regular assistance

### 4. Weatherization and Energy-Related Home Repairs
- Home improvements to reduce energy costs (insulation, weather stripping, furnace repair)
- Often coordinated with the Department of Energy's Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)

## Eligibility Requirements

### Income Guidelines

States must set income eligibility between certain federal boundaries:

- **Minimum threshold**: **110% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines (FPG)**
- **Maximum threshold**: **150% of the FPG** or **60% of the State Median Income (SMI)**, whichever is higher

### Income Limits by Household Size (2024-2025 Federal Poverty Guidelines)

| Household Size | 110% FPG | 150% FPG | 
|---------------|----------|----------|
| 1 | $17,215 | $23,475 |
| 2 | $23,265 | $31,725 |
| 3 | $29,315 | $39,975 |
| 4 | $35,365 | $48,225 |
| 5 | $41,415 | $56,475 |
| 6 | $47,465 | $64,725 |
| Each additional | +$6,050 | +$8,250 |

*Note: Actual state limits vary. Many states set their threshold at 150% FPG or 60% SMI. Contact your local LIHEAP office for exact limits.*

### Categorical Eligibility

Households may automatically qualify for LIHEAP if any household member receives benefits from:
- **SNAP** (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program / Food Stamps)
- **SSI** (Supplemental Security Income)
- **TANF** (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)
- **Certain Veterans' benefits** (means-tested)

### Priority Households

Federal law requires that states give priority to households with:
- The **highest energy costs** relative to income (highest energy burden)
- **Elderly members** (age 60 or older)
- **Disabled members**
- **Young children** (under age 6)

## Benefit Amounts

LIHEAP benefit amounts vary significantly by state and are based on:
- Household income and size
- Energy costs and type of fuel used
- Climate/weather conditions in the area
- Available funding

Typical benefit ranges:
- **Heating assistance**: $200–$1,000+ per household per season
- **Cooling assistance**: $100–$500 per household per season
- **Crisis assistance**: $200–$1,500 per incident

*Note: LIHEAP is not designed to cover the full cost of energy bills. It supplements household energy payments.*

## Required Documentation

To apply for LIHEAP, applicants typically need:
1. Proof of identity (government-issued ID for head of household)
2. Social Security numbers for all household members
3. Proof of income for all household members over age 18 (pay stubs, benefit letters, tax returns — usually for the past 30 days or most recent tax year)
4. Most recent energy/utility bill or fuel delivery receipt
5. Proof of residency (lease, mortgage statement, or utility bill)
6. Documentation of household composition (who lives in the home)
7. Proof of receipt of SNAP, SSI, or TANF benefits (if claiming categorical eligibility)

## Application Process

### How to Apply

- **Contact your local LIHEAP office**: Use the LIHEAP Clearinghouse locator at https://liheapch.acf.hhs.gov/locator
- **Apply through your local Community Action Agency** (CAA)
- **Call the National Energy Assistance Referral hotline**: 1-866-674-6327
- Some states offer online applications through their benefits portals

### Application Timeline

- **Heating season**: Applications typically accepted October through March
- **Cooling season**: Applications typically accepted May through September
- **Crisis assistance**: Available year-round in most states
- Processing time varies: typically 2–4 weeks for regular assistance, faster for crisis situations

## Funding and Availability

- LIHEAP is funded through annual federal appropriations, supplemented by occasional emergency funding
- **Annual funding**: Approximately $4–6 billion in recent years
- Funds are distributed to states based on a formula considering climate, low-income population, and residential energy costs
- Funds are limited and are distributed on a first-come, first-served basis in most states
- Not all eligible households receive assistance due to funding limitations — typically only 15-20% of eligible households are served

## Important Notes

- LIHEAP assistance does **not** need to be repaid — it is a grant, not a loan.
- You do not need to be behind on your energy bills to apply.
- Both renters and homeowners can apply, even if utilities are included in rent.
- Households in federally subsidized housing may still be eligible if they pay a portion of their energy costs directly.
- Immigration status of household members does not affect eligibility in most states — the focus is on the household's overall income.
- LIHEAP does not affect eligibility for other benefit programs.
