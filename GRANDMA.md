The AidNavigator AI is an application that helps people understand what government benefits or support programs they may be eligible for based on their personal situation. Instead of users trying to read long confusing policy documents, the system asks a few structured questions, checks relevant policy information, explains possible support in simple language and tells users how to apply for the benefits and the required documents needed for the application.
The application uses a Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture to ensure it avoids hallucinations, uses real policy documents data and enhances accuracy. When a user submits their profile, the process begins with a semantic search using Google Gemini Embeddings, which identifies precise paragraphs from official policy documents that apply to that specific individual’s situation. These relevant policy snippets are then injected into a private, structured prompt along with the user’s data. This context is handed over to the Llama 3 model (via Groq), which serves as the reasoning engine to compare the user's profile against the policy text, explaining exactly why they qualify and what steps they must take to apply. To maintain the highest level of security, every input and output passes through a custom safety guardrail layer that proactively detects jailbreak attempts, blocks fraud related content, prompt injections and automatically softens any overconfident language to ensure the results remain safe and advisory. All the technical stack used include: 
- The primary reasoning is handled by the Llama-3.3-70b model.
- Document mapping and vectorization are performed by Google Gemini’s models/embedding-001, ensuring semantic retrieval from the FAISS vector database.
- The entire flow is orchestrated using LangChain which manages the connection between the vector store, the model logic, and the prompt templates.
- The application is implemented using via a FastAPI backend, while the UI is provided through a Next.js frontend.
- Finally, the AI application is deployed using Vercel.
